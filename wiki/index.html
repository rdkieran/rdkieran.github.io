Alien Sarcophagus

The Alien Sarcophagus is an artefact discovered floating in space by Maddie and Charlie of the USS Voyeur. They brought it on board expecting some kind of terrible mystery to reveal itself, but it ended up being benign.

It's been on the ship for 2 years now, to no major event. It trembles and groans when it dries out, so people take turns moisturising it.

Rat Boy took a particular interest in it when he joined the crew with his dad, and took on primary moisturing duties. He has been thoroughly worn down by his frustration with it, and it's insistence on remaining a mystery.

Scans can't penetrate the casing, it seems that it was designed to withstand the void of space, and residue crusted on its surface reveals that it is incredibly old.

Something in there is alive, though, and so the USS Voyeur has been asked to take care of it and monitor it. It's thought that the Federation is just creeped out by it and doesn't want to keep it in any of their facilities, or maybe they've just got a backlog of artefacts to work through and don't have the space.

Allspecies™

The Allspecies™, commonly known as "The Collective" or "Whatchimacallits" is a biologically adaptive hivemind race of Frankenstein's monsters.

Where Holograms are mentally adaptive with limited physiologically, Allspecies™ are biologically adaptive, with limited mental abilities.

It is named for The Collective, a small faction of scientists which created it, after their original aims were corrupted by the influence of The Elven Conspiracy, in particular Laelithar and his family.

The development of the Allspecies™ began as an exercise in reversing death by Triple philosopher Brenda, and Troll geneticist Hayley. They sought funding through The Barket to establish a base of operations on The Party Planets. They enlisted the help of Bozo scientist Grundo, and others. Their funding came from Laelithar's family, working secretly within The Party Planets to develop technology to advance their interests in Conspiracy Space.

The key genetic components that allow the Allspecies™ to do its thing are Beeple collective thought, Thread spontaneous generation, and regenerative genetic technology developed by the Human scientist Ihava Fullbody.

Amy

Amy is a Human, serving The Galactic Federation of Consenting Planets as a communications officer on board the USS Voyeur.

She has served under Captain for several years, enjoying the highs and lows of the space mystery life. She is deadly with a rifle, with a quick temper when threatened.

On an away mission in the mines of Redworld IV, she was imprisoned by Clippers. While in a mental prison, she lucid dreamed herself aboard the USS Voyeur where she killed seven of her fellow Crewmates in a confused rage before she was released from captivity. Ever since, she's been quick to defend herself, and unsure of her reality.

History

She was born on Mars, in the slums of capital colony New Wick. She won a scholarship to study at Institute for Finding Things Out, where she was the only human in her year. She became friends with a Stork called Jockey, who was himself the only of his species that year.

Her first post following her graduation was at a communications outpost in deep space, facing The Void, where she worked with Bozo transmissions engineer Dorbobbie Dumbo.

They worked together in absolute silence for a year, monitoring signals from deep space, searching for signs of communication. One day, Amy received a signal. When she accidentally spoke aloud in excitement, she was quickly sent back to Federation Space for reassignment.

The outpost was subsequently quietly dismantled and quietly moved several thousands miles away, to avoid lingering noise pollution from her raised whisper, reverberating in space.

She still puzzles over the content of the message she received, which unfortunately left no trace on the system. She is unsure whether she saw it at all. She is haunted by it.

Beep Boop 4

Beep Boop 4 is a Portable Space Station owned by The Galactic Federation of Consenting Planets, currently orbiting the planet Termina IX, investigating its moon.

It briefly fell under control by the Elves during The Dabbling. Since then, it has remained a fringe vessel, investigating phenomena near the edge of Conspiracy space. Federation space stations have a kind of memory, known to engineers who work with them over a long time. Mechanical Sentience is a popular theory to account for the strange behaviours of ships in times of stress.

During the war, Beep Boop 4 seemed to resist occupation by The Elves. They lost control of the systems, the station malfunctioned and seemingly threatened to crush the Elves inside by shifting into it's contracted form. The Elves gave it up for unusable, but when engineers returned to the station after the war, they found the repairs necessary to fix the ship were easier than expected, and it was back in working order.

The crew of Beep Boop 4 are very attached to it.

The Admiral in charge of Beep Boop 4 currently is Admiral Dog.

The Bozos are comfortable. Their society is naively simple, and it seems to work with no effort whatsoever.

Biology

The goodwill of the Bozo people pleases the plant, and it creates a new Bozo in the image of the happy couple. It is picked from the tree and traditionally delivered by Stork.

Clippers

Clippers are an Unaligned Species that can be found in caves and moons in the Empty Sector, although they have been known to make their way further into Federation Space. No one really knows how they manage to get from planet to planet, but they're annoyingly persistent. They have a home planet where their society is based, but nobody's been there.

The Clippers that have left their home planet are scientists, probing alien species for testing. They capture explorers and put them through mostly harmless tests of skill and mental fortitude.

For instance, they put Amy through a dream test when she was captured on Redworld IV during an away mission with the USS Voyeur, in which she killed seven Crewmates before being awoken and rescued from captivity. The Clippers learned that humans are feral and dangerous when cornered.

It's thought that the Clippers share their findings with one another, and it's been noted that Clippers don't tend to make the same mistakes twice, and rarely repeat their experiments more than once. They don't do physical harm to their subjects most of the time, and this is thought to be a rule of theirs, although there are some cases where the Clippers have seemed to break this rule.

There is a small community of Clippers in The Party Planets who conduct social experiments with high people, like spreading rumors and starting cults.

They're annoying, and mostly people try to ignore them.

Dizbizazzle Bizzlebozzle 8 is the breakthrough fuel source manufactured by the Bozo. They marketed it as Blim Blam's Dizbizazzle Bizzlebozzle, and it was famous throughout the The Bozotopia System. The development of Dizbizzazzle Bizzlebozzle allowed the Federation to expand into further star systems, eventually leading to it's broad influence it enjoyed later.

Elf

Elves are the founders of The Elven Conspiracy, banded together as one people after their attempted forced assimilation into The Federation.

They come in many shapes and sizes, but as a rule, they always have big hands, and some kind of mystical abilities.

There are two main subspecies of Elf: Posh Elf and War Elf, and a third subspecies hunted to obscurity, the Yoga Elves. Of the three, the Posh Elves are the weakest in power, the War Elves are the weakest in will, and the Yoga Elves are the strongest in both, which the other two don't appreciate.

Posh Elf

Posh Elves, or Poshos, are the leader class of Elves. They are tall, dark haired, point-eared people with long, sharp fingers that form spade-like wedges on the end of their spindly arms.

They seem to float as they walk, and find a way to look down on everyone, even when they're lying on their back.

Powers

Posh Elves have some mystical ability to see into the minds of others and extract information. They do this through physical contact with the head, accessing memories, thoughts, and feelings.

War Elf

War Elves, aka Dwarves, are a more physically tough variant of Elf, with backgrounds in mining and technological knowledge.

They are big, square things with coarse black hair and heavy armoured suits. Many of them wear visors that subdue their latent psychic powers.

Powers

War Elves fear their powers, and have built their culture around their suppression. They are less compassionate, less emotionally vulnerable, for fear of the volatility of their psychic abilities.

Their visors suppress their powers, but without them, their powers would be dangerous to themselves and others. Their mental link to others is challenging to control, melding minds with others in a way that becomes difficult to untangle. Only Yoga Elves are capable of untangling themselves from the mind of a War Elf.

It's like a kind of reckless empathy that's uncomfortable to be stuck in, and difficult to escape. Except it does psychic damage.

Poshos look down on them for their lack of control over their abilities.

Yoga Elf

They have the same dark hair, pointy ears, and shovel-like hands, but tend to be short compared to the average Posh Elf. However, this makes little difference overall, and they can pass by unnoticed among other Posh Elves.

Because of their more potent abilities, Yoga Elves have been feared, hunted, and exiled by Posh Elf society for many years. As a result, they have a greater affinity for other species, which serves to reinforce the Posh Elves' latent xenophobia.

Powers

Yoga Elves share the Posh Elves' powers, but their abilities go beyond simply reading minds, but writing and rewriting them, and extracting the inner worlds of others into the physical realm.

This means that they can see and restructure someone's identity through mind manipulation in the case of the War Elf Ixtal, or extract past selves from someone's past and give them a physical form, as happened to Squeakella.

Hologram

Holograms are photonic entities generated by complex holomatrices. Most Federation facilities are fitted with hologram generators to accommodate the use of holograms as workers and assistants, although these holograms are typically contained to specific areas and limited in the scope of their programming.

Types of Hologram

There are three main types of hologram: Worker Hologram, Recreational Hologram, and Sentient Holograms.

Typically a hologram's ability to understand the world is limited. In the case of worker holograms, this is limited to their functional role, and for recreational hologram it is limited to an understanding of their context in the scenario they exist in.

For instance, USS Voyeur Crewmates Witch, until recently was the cackling villain of the village of Westerfield, which runs perpetually in the holodeck of the ship. Had she witnessed anything outside of the scope of middle ages Earth, she may not have been able to comprehend it.

However, Sentient Holograms do understand more. Some sentient holograms like Witch retain their dependency on computer systems, still being a quantifiable entity in the system, while other holograms like Vod were made biological.

Sentient Holograms

Witch changed from a Recreational Hologram stored in a Mental Block, to a Sentient Hologram following unknown conditions.

These kinds of glitches and transformations happen for a variety of reasons.

Interference with computer systems in Photonic Space, divine intervention, and maybe just a glitch are to blame. Sometimes they're just too well made, or not well made depending on your point of view.

Sentience & Mental Block

Let's get this straight. All holograms are sentient, or at least have the potential to be. A hologram like Witch has simply broken the limitations of her Mental Block to become cognizant of her reality, while Stork Hologram has not.

A Mental Block is a hologram's brain. It is is a section of unit of memory in Holodeck memory. Each object in a holographic space is one of these blocks. There are billions of blocks in a holo-generator, and so the number of reusable objects that can be created is effectively endless.

Different brands of mental block have different capabilities for forming their holograms. Recreational holograms like Witch utilise limiters designed for holonovel characters.

To ensure a holographic character cannot grow or become cognizant of its reality, it is assigned and limited to a certain set of variables inside of the Memory Block object. The holodeck's processor uses narrative logic to modify the objects to simulate cognizant thought. Narrative logic can change Mood variables and Posture variables, and the Narrative Logic dictates what conditions effect these variables in what way.

Villains may use alternative Narrative Logic to Heroes, for example, which guide the reactions within the Memory Block object.

However, Logic can act in unexpected ways when given access to extra variables. All Narrative Logic is adaptive, and so when presented with additional variables, it attempts to learn the correct reaction patterns based entirely on experience. This means someone like Witch may have villainous Narrative Logic, but her reactions to the world after she gained access to new variables in her Memory Block were chaotic and confused, until they settled on a winning strategy.

There are billions of potential variables, derived from AI learning sourced from hundreds of species.

Human

The Humiliation of Humans are a member species of The Galactic Federation of Consenting Planets with no official homeworld.

Earth, Mars, and so on

Some hundreds of years ago, they nuked the holy bejeezus out of the home planet Earth, and then they also nuked the holy bejeezus out of their backup planet of Mars.

The period of decline, destruction, and dispersal of the human race is known as The Humiliation. It's a bit mean, really.

They GFOCP would typically respond to cataclysmic events like this by terraforming a planet as a gift, but it was decided that it would be unwise to give the humans a third planet to destroy.

And so humans find themselves a nomadic people, scattered across space. It's said they can be found in groups in every populated system in the universe. Their invasive curiosity infects the universe.

Mean Spirited Little Creatures

Humans are a very lonely species. They lived too far away from all the wonders of the universe to enjoy them, and were purposefully excluded for hundreds of years. The Federation could have helped, humans could have been in the Federation in the 1800s. But following their investigations into human culture decided, they weren't worth the risk. Mean spirited little creatures.

It's hard to deny the reputation of humans when they made the movies themselves. Human movies quickly spread in the culture-starved Federation, which hadn't invited a new member species in decades. The movies linger on a lot of the negative aspects of human culture, and so it hasn't made the best impression on the rest of the galaxy.

It's hard for aliens to understand. They're from the majority part of space where it's bustling with life on every other planet. This was a fact that was obscured by Federation technology to keep Humans from getting too excited about going to space. Humans thought for years that they were alone in the universe, limited for too long to their lonely corner of the galaxy.

Uplifted Animals

Uplifted Animal are considered to be a sub-species of Human, being something like a genetically engineered parallel. After the humans were initially refused Federation membership, they got really lonely, and decided that if the aliens wouldn't have them, they could make aliens of their own. Humans still continue to create uplifted animals. It's something the Federation should really address at some point.

Laelithar

Laelithar is a Posh Elf, assigned to The Collective as an overseer on behalf of The Elven Conspiracy. He reports to his Sir.

History

After the departure of Hayley, Laelithar was assigned to keep the Collective project from falling apart due to management conflicts. The Humiliation is the period of Human history following on from the destruction of Earth involving the creation of Uplifted Animal, the destruction of most human colonies on Mars, and ultimately leading to the human race joining The Galactic Federation of Consenting Planets.

The human race, following the destruction of Earth by nuclear war, fell into a period of existential loneliness, feeling utterly alone in the universe with no promise of alien life to lift them out of their situation.

On the edge of extinction living in their flawed and oppressive Mars colonies, the few humans left longed for the comforts of Earth. Green grass, a dog to walk, a petting zoo, all of the funny little creatures now extinct by their catastrophic failure to preserve their planet.

Pulling from their genetic archives, the humans sought to create a kind of alien life for themselves, creating animal/human hybrids to give some colour to their dulling existence.

The GFOCP, surveying the humans' progress, decided to swoop in at this point to have a word with humans about their conduct.

Too Big

Too Big is the largest of the three planets that form the base of The Woods, alongside Too Small and Just Right. It is owned by The Bears, but run by The Bozo as a diplomatic base for The Federation.

History

Too Big, being the largest of the three planets, has the most space on its surface. It has few mountains, sparse foliage, and it's atmosphere is stretched too thin to comfortably settled.

As a result, it was mostly ignored by the Bears for much of their history.

However, as relations with the Bozo grew, Too Big was increasingly developed with terraforming technology as a diplomatic centre for the Bozos' newly formed Federation.

Metropolis

As the Federation grew, more and more of Too Big was set aside for colonies and communities for other Federation members, spanning across the planet's mostly flat surface. Above all, the trunk of the Skytree loom, now glowing with a million lights like a big Christmas tree, illuminating the city below.

The planet began to develop into something of a cultural centre for the whole Federation as more and more cultures set down roots there. Even some non Federation worlds developed communities on the surface of the planet, although under careful supervision.

As a result of this growing importance, Too Big has been involved in many of the major Federation events.

The Humiliation

The first major diplomatic upset for The Federation, The Humiliation was the unstable and risky inclusion of Humans and their Uplifted Animal genetically modified subspecies to The Federation.

Too Big opened up areas of the planet to house the Animal population, but controversially didn't extend this same courtesy to Humans, for fear of their instability and persisting love for weapons.

Humans instead were taken as refugees into various colonies across the planet, where they grew again in number and established themselves as an obnoxiously persistent presence in every society.

The Dabbling

After the discovery of Elves in deep space, The Federation embarked on a war to subjugate and take the Elves into their Federation. This poorly considered act became known as The Dabbling, and resulted in the establishment of The Elven Conspiracy, and the addition of the word Consenting to The Galactic Federation of Consenting Planets.

Too Big was thankfully left out of this war, with military might stored elsewhere in Bozotopia to keep Too Big safe from being targeted for retaliation.

There were protests. The many peoples of the planet disagreed with the invasion of the Elven worlds, and this pressure, as well as the Elves forces, compelled the Federation to cease their invasion. Most people were frankly annoyed that the Federation had forgotten that war is generally considered to be a no-no.

The Elves were allowed to settle on Too Big as a gesture of apology. Not many of them moved to these settlements, however.

Present Day

Too Big remains an important cultural and diplomatic centre for The Federation, but as it grows, and mistakes and disasters mark the Federation's reputation, the populace becomes increasingly divided

Too Small

Too Small is the smallest of the three planets that form the base of The Woods, alongside Too Big and Just Right. It is owned by The Bears, but is mostly ignored.

Small is a planet of extremes where it's said that lightning always strikes twice. Colossal mountains strike at the thick atmosphere, and deep oceans push down on the thin roots of its Skytree.

It's unknown how populated the planet is on account of its dense sprawl of manor houses, developed over thousands of years by old families living on the surface until it resulted in labyrinth of endless corridors.

Manor Sprawl

Too Small's extreme geography is woven with manor houses and architecture of huge variety, dense and endless, and very old. It's full of wonders and treasures which draw in adventurers, but few of them who go too deep can leave.

It's a slum, a rich slum full of rich people, who are lost and very far from civilization. Its extreme weather makes it impossible to approach by any kind of aerial shuttle, the only method of traversing it is through its network of manor houses and facilities. But as the sprawl expanded and condensed, the ways got obscured, and many people got lost in the halls. Few escape.

Teddie, full name Teddie Bear, is from one of these old families, and grew up in the Sprawl.

Triple

The Trip of Triples are a member species of The Galactic Federation of Consenting Planets from the Triad, a trio of planets called Planet I, Planet II, and Planet III

Biology

Triples have three legs. Sometimes biology makes mistakes. Triples are named for their tendency to trip, although most believe that they're named for their three legs. Related, but not quite right.

Triples spend much of their life doing their best to train their coordination to deal with having three legs. Learning to walk takes a lot longer for Triples, because there's more to consider than for bipedal species. As such, their young require longer-term care. Triples' cognitive development outpaces their motor skill development, as they spend a lot of time staring at the ceiling as babies, learning to do as much as they can with their hands, making them quite dexterous and introspective.

Essentially, talking is all that Triple babies can preoccupy themselves with. And with their naturally fast-thinking brains, they make fast progress on this front during early development.

It's not uncommon for Triples to learn how to speak before they can walk, and they are adept talkers. It's said that Triples developed their ability to chatter as a survival tactic, talking their way out of arguments with other babies, and out of fights with predators.

Federation Membership

The Trip fell into The Federation shortly before their official unification with the establishment of Too Big as their capital planet.

Triples were the most active members of SpaceNet in the centuries leading up to the establishment of the Federation. However, they were so chatty that their impact dominated the channels they engaged with, and a lot of the time they were talking between themselves as a result. Their late addition to the Federation clique is a result of their being largely ignored online, as their dominating conversational style was difficult for other species to keep up with.
